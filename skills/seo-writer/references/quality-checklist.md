# Quality checklist

Run this checklist after drafting, before presenting the final content to the user. This
covers AI pattern detection and general quality. Based partly on Wikipedia's "Signs of AI
writing" guide.

## AI writing patterns to catch and fix

These are the patterns that make content look machine-generated. Google's spam team knows
these patterns too, and so do readers.

### 1. Promotional inflation

**Watch for:** "comprehensive guide," "ultimate resource," "everything you need to know,"
"definitive," "game-changing," "revolutionary," "groundbreaking"

**Fix:** Delete these phrases entirely. Good content doesn't need to announce its own
quality.

### 2. Significance padding

**Watch for:** "stands as a testament to," "marks a pivotal moment," "plays a crucial/
vital/key role," "reflects broader trends," "underscores the importance of," "in today's
rapidly evolving landscape"

**Fix:** Replace with specific facts. "Marks a pivotal moment in AI development" becomes
"GPT-4 was released in March 2023 and was the first model to..."

### 3. Filler openers

**Watch for:** "In today's digital landscape," "In the ever-evolving world of," "When it
comes to [topic]," "In order to understand," "It's no secret that," "As we all know"

**Fix:** Delete the opener entirely. Start with the actual point.

### 4. AI vocabulary words

**Overused words to minimize:** Additionally, align, crucial, delve, emphasizing,
enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (as verb), interplay, intricate, key
(as adjective), landscape (metaphorical), leverage, moreover, multifaceted, navigate,
nuanced, pivotal, realm, robust, seamless, showcase, streamline, tapestry (metaphorical),
testament, transformative, underscore, utilize, vibrant

**Fix:** Use simpler, more specific alternatives. "Leverage" → "use." "Navigate the
landscape" → "deal with." "Robust solution" → describe what makes it robust.

### 5. Structural symmetry

**Watch for:** Every section is the same length. Every section has exactly 3 bullet
points. Every paragraph is 3 sentences. Headings follow an identical grammatical pattern.

**Fix:** Vary deliberately. Let one section be 2 paragraphs and the next be 5. Let some
sections have lists and others have none. Real writing is uneven.

### 6. Hedging pileups

**Watch for:** "may potentially help to possibly improve," "could be considered to
be somewhat important," "it's worth noting that this might suggest"

**Fix:** Pick one hedge or drop them all. "This may improve conversion rates" or "This
improves conversion rates." Not both simultaneously.

### 7. Em dash overuse

**Watch for:** More than one em dash per 500 words. AI text loves em dashes.

**Fix:** Replace most with commas, periods, or parentheses. Keep em dashes rare so they
have impact when you do use them.

### 8. Rule of three

**Watch for:** "speed, efficiency, and reliability," "innovate, collaborate, and grow,"
forced groupings of three where two or four would be more honest.

**Fix:** Use the actual number of things. If there are two points, list two. If there are
four, list four. Don't compress or pad to hit three.

### 9. Negative parallelisms

**Watch for:** "It's not just X — it's Y," "Not only does it X, but it also Y," "This
isn't merely X; it's a Y."

**Fix:** Just state what it is. "It's not just a tool — it's a platform" → "It's a
platform that includes X and Y."

### 10. Copula avoidance

**Watch for:** "serves as," "stands as," "functions as," "represents," "marks" used where
"is" or "are" would work fine.

**Fix:** Use "is" or "are." "The tool serves as a central hub" → "The tool is a central
hub."

### 11. Collaborative artifacts

**Watch for:** "I hope this helps," "Let me know if you have questions," "Great
question," "Here's an overview of..."

**Fix:** Delete. These are chatbot artifacts, not article content.

### 12. Sycophantic conclusions

**Watch for:** "The future looks bright," "Exciting times lie ahead," "This represents a
major step forward," "By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to..."

**Fix:** End with a specific action, a concrete takeaway, or a thought that lingers.

### 13. Bold-header lists

**Watch for:** Bullet lists where every item starts with a bolded label followed by a
colon, followed by a one-line explanation.

**Fix:** Convert to prose, or if a list is genuinely needed, vary the format. Not every
list needs headers.

## Quality checks (not AI-specific)

### Accuracy

- [ ] All statistics have a source (or are clearly marked as approximate)
- [ ] Named tools/products/people are spelled correctly
- [ ] Technical claims are accurate
- [ ] Pricing or feature information is current (or marked with date)
- [ ] Links point to the right pages

### Readability

- [ ] Flesch reading ease ~60-70 for general audience (adjust for technical content)
- [ ] No paragraph longer than 4 sentences
- [ ] Sentence length varies (mix of 8-word and 25-word sentences)
- [ ] Jargon explained on first use (for non-expert audiences)
- [ ] Active voice dominant (passive acceptable in technical or formal contexts)

### SEO verification

- [ ] Primary keyword in title (near the start)
- [ ] Primary keyword in meta description
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword density is sane (3-5 times per 1000 words max)
- [ ] Semantic keywords appear naturally throughout
- [ ] FAQ answers are snippet-length (40-60 words)
- [ ] Internal links included where specified
- [ ] No broken or placeholder links
- [ ] Meta description under 155 characters
- [ ] Title under 60 characters

### Content value

- [ ] Answers the search intent directly
- [ ] Includes something competitors don't (original angle, data, framework, examples)
- [ ] Reader would stay on this page rather than hit back and click another result
- [ ] CTA is clear and relevant (if applicable)
- [ ] Content is current and not outdated

## The final test

Read the entire piece start to finish. After reading, answer honestly:

1. **Would I share this?** If not, what's missing?
2. **Did I learn something or get something useful?** If not, where does it go generic?
3. **Could I tell this was AI-generated?** If yes, where are the tells?

If any answer is unsatisfying, revise those sections before presenting to the user.
